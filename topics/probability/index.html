PROBABILITY

The Operations Research techniques discussed in the preceding chapters assume that all the data are known with certainty. This assumption is not true always and may be far away from reality. For example, the demand for electric power during the summer months can vary randomly from year to year, depending on weather conditions. In such cases we use observed or historical data to describe the demand by a probability distribution.

Uncertainty And Probability

If it were possible to predict the future with complete certainty, the structure of the business world would radically be different from what it is. Had there been like uncertainty, there would be no excess production, no special clearance sales, no speculations on the stock market, etc. Unfortunately, we do not live in a world where failure could be predicted with complete certainty. Our desire to deal with uncertainty (e.g., while taking decision under uncertainty) leads us to what is known as 'Probability Theory'.

Experiment

A coin is tossed in the. A parachuter jumps from a plane. A ceiling fan is installed.

Outcome Of Interest

Coin lands with head up. His parachute will open in time. It will fall down.

Classical Definition Of Probability

If there are n exhaustive, pairwise mutually exclusive and equally likely outcome of a random experiment and if m out of these are favorable to an event A, then the probability of the happening of A, denoted by P(A), is defined by P(A)=m/n [0 ? m ? n, n > 0].

Note 1

This definition is on a priori knowledge of equally likely outcomes.

Note 2

This definition fails when the sample is infinite.
