Types of cloning vectors

A cloning vector is a small piece of DNA taken from a virus, a plasmid or the cell of a higher organism, that can be stably maintained in an organism and into which a foreign DNA fragment can be inserted for cloning purposes. These are 7 types of cloning vectors.

Plasmid

Plasmid is an autonomously replicating circular double stranded extra chromosomal DNA which is physically separated from a chromosomal DNA and can replicate independently.

Bacteriophage

1. The bacteriophages used for cloning are the phage ? and M13 phage. 2. Phage lambda is a bacteriophage or phage. Eg: bacterial virus, that uses E. coli as host. 3. M13 vectors are used for obtaining ss copies of cloned DNA which are suited for DNA sequencing and in vitro mutagenesis.

Cosmid

Cosmids are plasmids that incorporate a segment of bacteriophage ? DNA that has the cohesive end site (cos) which contains elements required for packaging DNA into ? particles.

Bacterial Artificial Chromosome(BAC)

BAC vectors are similar to standard E. coli plasmid vectors. It contain the origin and genes encoding the ori binding proteins required for plasmid replication.

Yeast Artificial Chromosome (BAC)

The yeast artificial chromosome (YAC) vector is capable of carrying a large DNA fragment (up to 200Kb), but its transformation efficiency is very low.

Human Artificial Chromosome (HAC)

1. Human artificial chromosome may be potentially useful as a gene transfer vectors for gene delivery into human cells. 2. It is a tool for expression studies and determining human chromosome function.

Retroviral Vectors

1. Retroviral vectors are used to introduce new or altered genes into the genomes of human and animal cells. 2. Retroviruses are RNA viruses.
