Applications of bioinformatics

Bioinformatics is information technology dealing with the maintenance and used of data in molecular biology using computers. It is being used many fields.

Molecular medicine

The human genome has several applications in the fields of biomedical research and clinical medicine. Every disease has a genetic component.

Personalized medicine

Clinical medicine will become more personalized with the development of the field of pharmacogenomics. It is the study of how an individual's genetic inheritance affects the body's response to drugs.

Preventative medicine

The genetic mechanisms of disease are not known, the development of diagnostic test to measure a person's susceptibility to different disease is hardly possible. Preventative actions such as change of lifestyle or having precautionary treatment at the earliest possible in our struggle to conquer disease.

Gene therapy

Gene therapy is the treatment of genetic diseases by changing the expression of a person's gene.

Drug development

At present all drugs on the market target only about 500 proteins. More knowledge of proteomics and disease mechanisms enable us to identify and validate new drug targets, on which the drugs act selectively.

Microbial genome applications

Microbes are determined by their genomes. It's capacities have broad and far reaching implications on environment, health, energy and industrial applications.

Waste cleanup

1. Deinococcus radiodurans is the world's toughest bacterium and it is most radiation resistant organism. 2. Database of microbial diversity is useful for screening such useful microbes.

Climate change studies

Extensive use of fossil as energy source is the major cause the carbon dioxide pollution in the atmosphere.

Alternative energy sources

Scientists are studying the genome of the microbe chlorobium tepidum. It has unusual capacity for generating energy from light, as an alternative energy source.

Biotechnology

Biotechnology database offers much more information about microbial biodiversity and usefulness of different microbes that can be used in biotechnology industry.

Antibiotic resistance

Genomics databases provides fine details about the genetic control of antibiotics resistance and mode of its transmissions.

Bioweapon creation

Genomics databases provide complete information for artificial synthesis of viruses.

Evolutionary studies

The sequencing of genomes from all three domains of life, eukaryota, bacteria and archaea, means that evolutionary studies can be performed.

Veterinary science

Sequencing projects in farm animals including cows, pigs and sheep will provide a better understanding of the biology of these animals.
