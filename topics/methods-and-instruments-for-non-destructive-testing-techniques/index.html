Methods and instruments for non destructive testing techniques

NDT used in science and technology industry to evaluate the properties of a material,component or system without causing damage.

Major 8 types of NDT methods:

Ultrasonic testing Visual Testing Eddy current testing Magnetic particle testing Acoustic emission testing Liquid penetrant testing Leak testing Radiography testing

Ultrasonic testing:

It is based on the propagation of ultrasonic wave in the object or material tested.

Visual testing:

Visual testing is inspection for flaws that are visible to the naked eye and used to detect visible flaws such as deformation ,welding defects and corrosion.

Eddy current testing:

It is based on the principle of generating circular electrical currents (eddy currents) in a conductive material.

Magnetic particle testing:

The principle of the method is that the specimen is magnetised to produce magnetic lines of force, or flux, in the material.

Acoustic emission testing:

It detects and monitors the release of ultrasonic stress waves from localised sources when a material deforms under stress.

Liquid penetrant testing:

Liquid penetrant testing used to check for deficiencies such as cracks by spreading a thin liquid dye onto the material's surface, followed by the application of a developer

Leak testing:

It is used to determine if an object, product, or system functions within a specified leak limit.

Radiography testing

It is based on the principle that radiation is absorbed and scattered as it passes through an object.

List of NDT equipments:

Ultrasonic flaw detector Ultrasonic thickness measurement Eddy current tester Magnetic particle testing machine Radiography testing equipment Thickness gauges Flaw detector Material condition tester and Devices that measure conductivity ,resistivity,corrosion
