Types of jigs

A jig is a work-holding device that holds, supports, and locates the workpiece and guides the one or more tools to perform a specific operation.

Types of jigs:

1.Template jig , 2.Plate jig , 3.Diameter jig , 4.Channel jig , 5.Ring jig , 6.Box jig , 7.Leaf jig , 8.Angle plate jig , 9.Indexing jig , 10.Trunnion jig .

Template jigs:

1.Temple jigs are normally used for accuracy rather than speed, 2.It is the least expensive , 3.Simplest type of jigs .

Plate jigs:

1.It is the modified form of the template jig with jig bushes incorporated on the temple, 2.They use a flat plate containing bushing as their main structural member.

Diameter jig:

Diameter jig is used to drill radial holes on a cylindrical or spherical workpiece.

Channel jig :

Channel jig is a simple type of jig having a channel-like cross section.

Ring jig :

1.Ring jig is employed to drill holes on circular flanged parts. 2.The work is securely clamped on the drill body, 3.The holes are drilled by guiding the tool through drill bushes.

Box jig:

1.Number of holes can be drilled economically with box jig 2.The workpiece and saves times and increase production.

Leaf jig:

Leaf jig has a leaf which may be swung open or closed on the work for loading or loading purposes.

Angle plate jig:

1.Used to machine parts at right angles to their locators 2.Pulleys and Gears use this type of jig

Indexing jig:

Indexing jigs are used to accurately space holes or other machined areas around a Part.

Trunnion jig:

These jigs are used for drilling holes on very large or odd.
