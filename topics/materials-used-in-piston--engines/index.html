Materials used in piston engines

Piston material and design contribute to the overall durability and performance of an engine.

Piston materials:

The most commonly used piston materials are 1.cast Iron 2.Aluminum alloys

1.cast Iron:

Cast iron pistons are used for moddderately rated engines with piston speed below 6m/s.

2.Aluminium alloys:

Alluminium alloys pistons are used for highly rate engines with piston speed above 6m/s.

Cast Iron characteristics:

1.Its is hard 2.Brittle 3.Non-mellable 4.very good in compression

Aluminum alloys characteristics:

1.High operating temperature 2.outstanding corrosion resistance 3.lightweight 4.High electrical conductivity 5.Excellent thermal conductivity

Advantages of aluminium over cast iron:

1.High thermal conductivity (approx 3 times that of cast iron). 2.Less variation in temperature from the crown to the piston engines. 3.Density of aluminium is aboutone third that of cast iron
