Principle,Merits,Demerits,Types and application of Forging and Rolling

Forging is shaping of metal using localized compressive forces

Merits of forging:

1. Stronger. 2. More reliable and less costly. 3. Better response to heat treatment. 4.more consistent and better metallurgical properties. 5.Requires fewer secondary operations. 6.Great design flexibility.

Demerits of forging:

1.In hot forging it is difficult to perform secondary operations 2.Capital cost is more 3.Very high man and material safety procedures need to be followed

Common Applications for Forging:

1.Automobile industry 2.Valves and fittings 3.Hand tools and hardware 4.Machinery and equipment 5.Defense 6.Aerospace

Types of froging:

Hot forging,cold forging

Rolling:

Shaping of metals into semi-finished or finished forms by passing between rollers rotating in opposite direction.

Merits of Rolling:

1.Uniform dimensions 2.Close tolerance 3.High-speed production

Demerits of rolling:

1.The cost of equipment is high. 2.Suitable for large scale production only. 3.Poor surface finish

Application of Rolling:

1.Rods, seamless hollow tubes are made by rolling, 2.Cutting the gears on the gear blank, 3.The threaded parts, bolts, screws, etc. 4.In automotive industries, various parts are manufactured by the rolling process, 5.Used to made plates, steel sheets, etc, 6.Bearing, Turbines rings are rolling products.

Types of rolling:

1.Thread/Gear Rolling. 2.Shape Rolling. 3.Ring Rolling. 4.Tube Piercing. 5.Transverse Rolling/Roll Forging. 6.Skew Rolling. 7.Roll Bending. 8.Flat Rolling.

Principle of Rolling:

1.Passing the metal through a gap between rollers rotating in opposite direction. 2.Rollers compress the metal while simultaneously shifting it forward because of the friction at the roller-metal interfaces.
