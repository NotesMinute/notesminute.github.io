Balloon flight,ornithopters,early airplanes by wright brothers

Balloon flight,ornithopters are early flying vehicles discovered by Montgolfier brothers (Joseph and Jacques),Jacob Degen

List of 12 Early Flying Machines:

1.Aerial Screw (Helicopter) 2.The First Hot Air Balloon 3.Dumont Personal Airship No. 6 4.Degen Ornithopter 5.Phillips Multiplane 6.Aerial Steam Carriage 7.Lilienthal Hang Glider 8.1896 Chanute Glider 9.1804 Cayley Glider 10.Whitehead No. 21 11.14-bis 12.Wright Flyer

1.Aerial Screw (Helicopter):

1.In Fifteenth century 2.By Leonardo da Vinci 3.It looks like the modern helicopter

2.The First Hot Air Balloon:

1.In 18th century 2.By the Montgolfier brothers (Joseph and Jacques) 3.The first manned hot air balloon took flight in the same year on 21 November in Paris, with Jean-François Pilâtre de Rozier and François Laurent d’Arlandes on board.

3.Dumont Personal Airship No. 6:

1.Built in 1901 2.By the Brazilian aviation pioneer Santos-Dumont

4.Degen Ornithopter:

1.Built In 1807 2.By Jacob Degen 3.It was a flying machine with wings attached to it

5.Phillips Multiplane:

1.Designed in 1880s 2.Designed and built by Horatio Phillips 3.It is the first unmanned multiplane

6.Aerial Steam Carriage:

1.Designed in 1842 2.By William Samuel Henson 3.It had a monoplane design with 150 feet wing

7.Lilienthal Hang Glider:

1.Designed in 1891 2.By Otto Lilienthal 3.It was in monoplane, biplane, and bat-wing forms

8.1896 Chanute Glider:

1.Built in 1896 2.By Octave Chanute, William Avery 3.It was a hang glider with a biplane design

9.1804 Cayley Glider:

1.Designed In 1804 2.Designed by Sir George Cayley 3.It was a monoplane glider

10.Whitehead No. 21:

1.Built in 1901 2.Built by Gustave Whitehead 3.It was a monoplane aircraft

11.14-bis:

1.Built in 1906 2.Built by Santos-Dumont 3.The 14-bis was a biplane aircraft

12.Wright Flyer:

1.Built in 1903. 2.Designed by the Wright brothers (Wilbur and Orville). 3.It was a biplane aircraft

Balloon flight:

The hot air caused the balloons to become lighter than air and rise off the ground, achieving flight.

Ornithopters:

It flies by flapping its wings. Look like birds,insects

Applications of unmanned ornithopters:

Military applications for surveillance purpose
