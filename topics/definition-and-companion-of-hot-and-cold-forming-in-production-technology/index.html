Definition and companion of hot and cold forming in production technology

Forming is give a shape of a partly finished product

Hot forming:

1.combination of extruding and rolling 2.Hot working is done at higher temperatures

Advantages of hot forming:

1.Higher ductility , 2.Lower flow stress , 3.Smaller grain size , 4.Stronger, tougher and more ductile , 5.Metal that usually fracture in cold working can be hot formed.

Disadvantages of hot forming:

1.Surface reactions between metal and the furnace atmosphere. (i.e. oxidation) , 2.Hot shortness, 3.Dimension tolerance, 4.Handling is more difficult (from furnace to machine).

Cold forming :

1.combination of extruding and rolling 2.cold working is done at room temperatures

Advantages of cold forming:

1.Better dimensional control , 2.Strength and hardness of the metal increased, 3.Easier handling (low operating temperature) , 4.Contamination problems are minimized , 5.No heating of work required.

Disadvantages of cold forming:

1.Higher forces are required, 2.Heavier, more powerful and stronger equipments are required, 3.Ductility and strain hardening limit the amount of forming that can be done, 4.Undesirable residual stresses may be produced.

Differences:

1.Hot working 2.cold working

1.Hot working:

1.Above recrystallization temperature. 2.Formation of new crystals. 3.Surface finish is not good. 4.No residual stresses. 5.Less force is required.

2.cold working:

1.Below recrystallization temperature. 2.No formation of crystals. 3.Surface finish is good. 4.Formation of Residual stresses . 5.More force is required.
