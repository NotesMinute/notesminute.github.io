CAN(Controller Area Network)

1.CAN stands for Controller Area Network. 2.It is one of the popular networks implemented in modern automotive systems for better connectivity and communication between different Nodes or ECU(Electronic Control Units). 3.Each nodes in the system are connected by CAN bus.

CAN bus physical layer

1.In more technical terms the controller area network is described by their data link layer and physical layer. 2.In HighSpeed CAN ISO11898-1 ,say about the data link layer. 3.ISO11898-2,says about the physical layer. 4.CAN bus physical layer defines things like cable type to be used,the level of electrical signal,node requirements to be taken care and the impedance of cable etc. •Baudrate-Typically 1Mbit/sec(Classical CAN )and 5Mbit/sec(HighSpeed CAN). •Cable Length-maximum length of cable can be between 500 meters(125 Kbit/sec)and 40 meters(1Mbit/sec). •Termination-Proper termination of 120 ohms(Termination resistor) at each end of the bus.

Advantages of CAN

1.Simple and low cost in terms of cabling which requires only two wires to cable entire systems. 2.It helps to communicate to entire nodes through one point of entry as it is fully centralized-enables easy dignostics,data logging and configuration. 3.Extremely robust in terms of interference from electrical disturbances and electromagnetic interference,which makes it ideal for critical applicationsas in case of in-vehicle communications. 4.Efficient because priority is given for those data which are critical for proper system function ,without causing any disturbances to other frames.
