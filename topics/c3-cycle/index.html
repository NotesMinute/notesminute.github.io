C3 cycle

Calvin cycle is a cyclic reaction occuring in the dark phase of photosynthesis. In this reaction CO2 is converted into sugar and hence it is a process of carbon fixation.

Other name of calvin cycle

1. Dark reactions, 2. C3 cycle, 3. Reductive pentose phosphate cycle, 4. Photosynthetic carbon reduction cycle.

Stages of C3 cycle

1. Fixation: In the stroma , in addition to CO2, two other components RUBISCO and RUBP enzymes are present to initiate the light independent reaction. 2. Reduction: 6 molecules of 3 PGA are phosphorylated by 6 molecules of 1,3 diphosphoglyceric acid and 6 molecules of ATP. Its reduced the use of molecules of NADPH2. 3. Regeneration: In the regeneration phase, the ribose diphosphate is regenerated into one molecule of ribulose 5 phosphate.

Significance of C3 cycle

1. It is the main biochemical pathway during the dark reaction of photosynthesis. 2. It stores the ATP energy formed during light reaction in the carbohydrates molecules as food energy. 3. It is the primary source of organic food energy for all the organisms.
