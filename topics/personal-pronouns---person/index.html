Personal pronouns - person

Grammatical person refers to the perspectives of the personal pronouns used to identify a person in speech and text—that is, it distinguishes between a speaker (first person), an addressee (second person), and others beyond that (third person).

first person rule 1

First-person pronouns are used to express an autobiographical point of view —they tell what is directly happening to the speaker, writer, or fictional character

first person rule 2

When the speaker is part of a group, the first-person pronouns inflect to the plural

second person rule 1

We use the second-person pronouns to indicate those who are being addressed directly by the speaker

econd person rule 2

Unlike first-person pronouns, there is not a distinction between singular and plural second-person pronouns (except in the reflexive form)

third person rule 1

Third person is used to talk about someone or something that is not the speaker and is not being directly addressed. This is most widely used in fiction writing

third person rule 2

When the person or thing is singular, the pronouns used in the third person are the different forms of she, he, and it

third person rule 3

Sometimes, when a single person of an unknown gender is being discussed, the third-person plural forms (they, them, etc.) are used as a gender-neutral alternative to the third-person feminine/masculine forms
