Classification of welding process

The four main types of welding are: 1.Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW/MIG), 2.Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (GTAW/TIG), 3.Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW), 4.Flux Cored Arc Welding (FCAW).

Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW/MIG):

GMAW is produced by an electric arc between the base metal and a solid wire continuously fed electrode using a shielding gas. Also called MIG welding (Metal Inert Gas)

Advantages of GMAW:

All Position Welding Quality Welds, Little to No Slag, Low Spatter, Variety of Metals Various Material Thickness, High Efficiency, High Radiated Heat.

Limitations of GMAW:

Cost, Portability, Outdoor Welding, Clean Base Material, Potential for Cold Lap, Potential for Undercut, High Radiated Heat.

Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (GTAW/TIG):

Arc welding process that uses a non consumable tungsten electrode to produce the weld. The filler metal is added from an external source, usually as bare metal filler rod.

Advantages of GTAW:

Superior quality welds, generally free from spatter, porosity, or other defects Precise control of arc and fusion characteristics , Weld almost all metals , Used with or without filler wire, Easily automated , Used in all positions, Intricate geometries weldable.

Limitations of GTAW

Difficulty, Speed, Non-Automation, Cost.

Shielded metal arc welding:

Manual arc welding process that uses a consumable electrode covered with a flux to lay the weld.

Advantages of SMAW:

Simple, portable and inexpensive equipment, Wide variety of metals, welding positions and electrodes are applicable, Suitable for outdoor applications.

The Disadvantages of SMAW:

1.Is not automated and thus usually has lower productivity rates than automated processes. 2.Deposition rates are typically lower than for other welding processes. 3.Requires more operator skill than many other processes.

Flux Cored Arc Welding (FCAW):

1 Semi-automatic arc welding process 2.FCAW uses a continuous wire fed electrode, a constant-voltage welding power supply, and similar equipment to MAG welding.

Disadvantages of FCAW:

1.A high level of noxious fumes which must be ventilated. 2.Higher electrode wire cost compared to solid electrode wires. 3.More costly equipment than many other welding processes.

Advantages of FCAW:

1.Yields high quality, consistent welds with fewer defects. 2.A high deposition rate, which is the speed that the filler metal is applied. 3.Can be used in all positions with the right filler metal. 4.Suitable for outdoor welding or shop welding.
