Important factors affecting combustion chamber design

The design of combustion chamber of gas turbine engine is different that conventional combustion devices.space,combustion intensity,faster combustion process are major requirements

Temperature of gases

The temperature of the burned gases must be comparatively low to suit highly stressed turbine materials.The temperature may be allowed to rise if improved materials and blade cooling methods are adopted

Temperature distribution

At the end of combustion chamber,the distribution of temperature along the turbine annulus,influences the allowable from root to tip.Therefore,temperature distribution need not be uniform

Combustion

Design should be such that combustion is maintained in stream of air moving with a high velocity approximately of 30-60 m/s

Carbon deposits

Carbon deposits are hard brittle materials,may damage turbine blades,block cooling air passage,change the flow pattern,or any severe change may result

Combustion chamber pressure

Combustion stability is also a function of pressure.combustion must be stable over wide range of chamber pressure

Volume and weight

Volume and weight of the combustor directly affects the overall size and weight of the power plant
