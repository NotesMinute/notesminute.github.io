Main Rotors System

Three clasification

The main rotor system is classified according to how the main rotor blades are attached and move relative to the main rotor hub. There are three basic classifications: 1) Semi-rigid 2) Rigid 3) Fully Articulated

Semi-Rigid Rotor System

1) It is usually composed of two blades that are rigidly mounted to the main rotor hub. 2) It is free to tilt for the main rotor shaft on what is know as a teetering hinge. 3) This allows the blades to flap together as a unit. 4) As one blade flaps are facing upwards and the other one facing on downwards. 5) This system is capable of featuring, which means that the pitch angle of the blade changes. 6) To reduce the lead and lag forces the rotor system is mounting slightly lower than the usual plane of rotation. 7) Its center of gravity (CG) is below where it is attached to the mast. 8) Helicopter with this type of system are unsafe to a condition known as mast bumping which case flaps stops to shear the mast. 9) Mast bumping is the result of excessive rotor flapping. 10) In straight and level flight, blade flapping is minimal, perhaps 2 degree under usual flight condition.

Rigid Rotor System

1) It is a rare design but potentially offers the best properties of both fully articulated offer the best properties o both the fully articulated and semi-rigid rotors. 2) Here the blade roots are rigidly attached to the rotor hub. 3) The blade does not have hinges to allow lead-lag or flapping. 4) Instead, the blade accommodates theses motions by using elastomeric bearings. 5) Elastomeric bearing is moulded, rubber-like materials that are bonded to the appropriate parts. 6) Instead of rotating like conventional bearings, they twist and flex to allow proper movement of the blades.
