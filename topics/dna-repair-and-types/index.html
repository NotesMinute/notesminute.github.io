DNA repair and types

DNA repair refers to the number of processes by which a cell identifies and corrects damage to the DNA molecules that encode its genome. Depending on the type of damage inflicted on DNA‘s double helical structure, a variety of repair strategies have evolved to restore lost information. There are 4 repair types.

Direct repair

1. This system act directly on damaged nucleotides and convert each one back to its original structure.But only a few damaged nucleotides can be repaired directly. 2. Pyrimidine dimers are repaired by a light-dependent direct system called photoreactivation.

Excision repair

1. It involves excision of a segment of the polynucleotide containing a damage site, followed by resynthesis of the correct nucleotide sequence by a DNA polymerase. 2. Excision ? Resynthesis ? Ligation. 3. Excision repair is of two types: a) Base excision repair, b) Nucleotide excision repair.

Mismatch repair

Mismatch repair corrects errors of DNA replication and recombination that results in mispaired but undamaged nucleotides.

Recombinational repair

1. Recombinational repair requires the presence of an identical or nearly identical sequence to be used as a template for repair of the break in the DNA strand. 2. The enzymatic machinery responsible is nearly identical to the machinery responsible for chromosomal crossover during meiosis.
