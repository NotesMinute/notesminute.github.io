Troubleshooting and rectification procedures of jet engines

Troubleshooting is a form of problem solving, often applied to repair failed products or processes on a machine or a system

Troubleshooting and rectification procedures done by the following steps

1.Conduct a visual Inspection 2.Conduct a operational check 3.Classify the trouble 4.Isolate the trouble 5.Locate the trouble 6.Correct the trouble 7.Conduct a final operational check

1.Conduct a visual Inspection:

This inspection should be through and searching -checking all lines,units,mechanical linkage and components for evidence of leaks,looseness,security,material condition,and proper installation.

2.Conduct a operational check:

1.This is normally acomplished by attaching the support equipment to the aircraft. 2.The AM checks for external leakage , the correct direction of component movement,its proper sequence of operation,speed.

3.Classify the trouble:

1.Malfunctions usually fall into four basic categories hydralic,pneumatic,mechanical,electrical. 2.something affecting normal flow of hydralic fluid would be classified under the hydrlic classification. 3.Somthing affect the normal flow of compressed gases is classified as a pneumatic malfunction. 4.Mechanical discrepancies should be found during visual inspections.

4.Isolate the trouble:

1.During this step ,you must use your knowledge and known facts to determine where the malfunction exists in the system. 2.Usually the trouble can be pinned down to one or two areas.

5.Locate the trouble:

1.This step is used to eliminate unnecessary parts removal, thus saving money, valuable time, and man-hours. 2.Internal leaks may occur in valves, actuators, or other hydraulic units.

6.Correct the trouble:

1.This step is accomplished only after the trouble has been definitely located and there is no doubt that your diagnosis is correct. 2.Mal-functions are usually corrected by replacement of units or components, rigging and adjustments, and bleeding and servicing.

7.Conduct a final operational check:

The affected system must be actuated a minimum of five times, or until a thorough check has been made to determine that its operation and adjustments are satisfactory.
