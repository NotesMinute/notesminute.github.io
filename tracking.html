Tracking

5 methods.

Giriprabhu Seshadri

Stick method
A wick and stick is used; with the helicopter operating at specific rpm, the wick attached to the stick will be plaed in contact with the rotor blades; blades are checked for marks; if both the blades are marked ame amount~ the blades are in track.

Flag method
Flag is covered with cloth, withmasking tape on the outer edge. Helicopter operates at an rpm~ and flag is rotated in tip path of the rotor. This will leave notches in the oute edge of the flag with the color on the edge of the notch which decides the alignment

Light reflector method
Reflectors are plaaced on the blade tip of the helicopter facing will be plain while the outer will have as strip placed across the middle; with a spot light reflection the alignment is checked.

Pretrack method
The blade is matched to a master blade and preflown in a spin test. The pitch change rods is set in predetermined length. When a new blade is installed, the correct length is set on the rod and blade is in track.

Electronic strobe
Reflectors are placed on the tips of the blades facing the cabin. The strobe light is used to view the refector. The strobe is triggered by an interrupter and pick-up mounted on swashplate, the light is reflected from swash plate and checked for track
