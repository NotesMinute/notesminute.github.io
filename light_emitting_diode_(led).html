Light Emitting Diode (LED)

A solid state device that converts electrical energy into single color light.

Prathipa Ravichandran

Construction of LED
"LED consists of a PN junction surrounded by a hard

transparent plastic epoxy resin hemispherical shaped body."
"Construction of epoxy resin body helps in reflecting away the emitted photons of light from the surrounding substrate base

thus focusing upwards through the top"
"The cathode and anode terminal of an LED is normally identified by a notch

or by one lead shorter than the other."
LED Operation

Semiconductor diode that emits light when a current flows from anode to cathode across the PN junction of the device.
LED requires a direct current supply to forward bias the junction with a positive voltage for normal operation.

Voltage and current in LED is inversely proportional - so the LED turns on at a lower voltage and will rapidly draw much higher current as the voltage increases.
Advantages of LED

Small in size - Durable and resistance to shock and vibration - Very fast on-time - Good color resolution - Can be powered from a portable battery
Disadvantages of LED

Unreliable for outdoor applications with great temperature variations - Employ large heat sinks to protect semiconductors from heat damage

"Traffic lights, Indication lights on devices
