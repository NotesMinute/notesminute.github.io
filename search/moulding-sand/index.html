Moulding Sand

Properties of Moulding Sand

1) Porosity or Permeability:- * Molten metal contact with moisture sand, generate steam or vapor and lead to the formation of gas holes or pores. *Allow gases or moisture present in a mould to be removed. 2) Flowability:- * Ability to behave like a fluid. * Park all around the pattern and take the required shape. 3) Collapsibility:- * molten metal solidifies the sand, mould must be collapsible.so free contraction of metal occurs. 4) Adhesiveness:- * Sand particles should be adhering to another body. *Attach to the sides of moulding box. 5) Cohesiveness or Strength:- * Ability of sand particles stick together. * Insufficient strength loads to collapse of mould. * Sand should strong enough and permit the mould to be formed in the desired shape. 6) Refractoriness:- * Capable of withstanding the high temperature of the molten without fusing. * It is measured by sinter point (comparting and forming a solid mass of material by heat or pressure without melting.
