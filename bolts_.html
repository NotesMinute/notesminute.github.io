Types of Nuts

Castle Nut
Nut with slots (notches) cut into one end. The name comes from the nut's resemblance to the crenellated parapet of a medieval castle

Acron Nut
Acorn nut is its common name and it is also called a crown hex nut| blind nut| cap nut or domed cap nut. It is a nut that has a domed top to cover the threads

Hex Jam Nut
Hex jam nuts are thinner than a standard hex nut but have the same dimension across the flats and corners

Hex Jam Nylock Nut
The nylon material located at the top of the nut prevent loosening from vibration. Nylon Insert Jam Nuts in this selection are made of 304 Stainless Steel suitable for corrosion resistance in applications exposed to fresh water

Hex Nut
Nuts have internal machine threads| for use with machine thread bolts and screws

K Nut
The K-nuts are lightweight| self-locking and with a reduced hex. The fixed anchor nuts and floating anchor nuts are both rivet mounted.

Hex Nylock Nut
A nyloc nut| also referred to as a nylon-insert lock nut| polymer-insert lock nut| or elastic stop nut| is a kind of locknut with a nyloncollar insert that resists turning

Square Nut
A square nut is a four-sided nut. Compared to standard hexnuts| square nuts have a greater surface in contact with the part being fastened| and therefore provide greater resistance to loosening

Wing Nut
Nut with a pair of projections for the fingers to turn it on a screw

Flange Nut
A flange nut is a nut that has a wide flange at one end that acts as an integrated| non-spinning washer. This serves to distribute the pressure of the nut over the part being secured| reducing the chance of damage to the part and making it less likely to loosen as a result of an uneven fastening surface
