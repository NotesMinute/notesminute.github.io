Working of Combustion chamber

Air goes in the front| passes through a bunch of compressor blades| gets compressed| is lead into the combustion chamber.A mixture of fuel and the compressed air is ignited.Creates an immense amount of pressure which is then passed through to the turbine blades at the back of the engine that spin and create thrust

Types of cooling techniques used in turbine blade
Internal cooling , External cooling

Internal cooling
Convection cooling , Impingement cooling

External cooling
Film cooling , Cooling effusion ~Pin fin cooling ~Transpiration cooling

Convection cooling
It works by passing cooling air through passages internal to the blade

Impingement cooling
Works by hitting the inner surface of the blade with high velocity air.

Film cooling
Consists of pumping the cooling air out of the blade through multiple small holes in the structure

Cooling effusion
The blade surface is made of porous material which means having a large number of small orifices on the surface.

Pin fin cooling
There is an array of pin fins on the blade surface. Heat transfer takes place from this array and through the side walls

Transpiration cooling
"It creates a thin film of cooling air on the blade| but it is different in that air is ""leaked"" through a porous shell rather than injected through holes"
