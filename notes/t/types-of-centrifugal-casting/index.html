Types of centrifugal casting

True centrifugal casting.

* Molten metal is poured while the mould rotates at its axis. * May be horizontal,vertical or inclined ( 0 to 90 degree ). * Horizontal axis of rotation is more common. * Outside of mould is covered with water coolant to accelerate solidification. * Proper control of flow rate and pouring orifice, long and large tubes of uniform quality is obtained.

Advantages of true centrifugal casting.

* Casting acquire the high density and are distinguished for their fine-grained structure and high mechanical strength. * Inclusions and impurities such as oxide, slag, and gas, etc, being lighter than the molten metal will segregate towards the center to the inside of the casting, where they may be removed by a subsequent machining operation. * Gates and risers are not needed which results in saving in material and increasing the yield. * High output. * Formation of hollow interiors without cores. * The casting is less subject to directional variation than static castings.

Disadvantages of true centrifugal casting.

* Contamination of inner surface of the casting makes it necessary to increase finishing allowance for subsequent machining of the inner surface. * An inaccurate diameter of the surface of a casting. * Not all the alloys can be cast by this method.

Application of true centrifugal casting.

* Water supply * Sewerage pipes * Steel gun barrels * Chemical reactor vessels * Pressure vessel bodies * Reactor tubes * Piping for nuclear power plants * paper mill rolls * Textile rolls, etc. Keynotes: * The method of a true centrifugal vertical machine which is also known as "The deLavaud Process".

Semi Centrifugal casting

* It is a process used to produce solid casting and hence it requires a core to produce hollow cavities. * Forming symmetrical shaped objects and axis of rotation is vertical. * High pouring pressure is not produced and the impurities are not rejected towards the center.

Application of Semicentrifugal casting

* This process is used for making wheels, rings, rollers, pulleys, flywheels, gear blank, Turbo supercharge diaphragm disk, steel railroad wheels, nozzles and similar parts etc.

Centrifuge casting

* When the group of small molds is arranged in a circle (to balance each other) around the central vertical axis of the vertical axis, this process is called centrifuge casting. * It is clear that the molds are not symmetrical about the axis of rotation, that is, the axis of casting and the axis of rotation do not coincide with one another. *Here again the centrifugal force is used to obtain higher pressure on the metal and get more dense casting. * The molten metal will flow to all the molds under centrifugal force from a central feeding sprue.
